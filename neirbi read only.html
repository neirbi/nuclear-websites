Why this is different

You are buying future customers.

Calling a service business is effort. People are busy. Finding someone, making the call, being sold, it's uncomfortable.

They only call when the discomfort exceeds calling. Until that threshold is crossed, they tolerate it.

They notice. They forget. They do nothing. Problems sit in the back of their mind for weeks. Months. Life is busy. It's not a today problem.

Until it disrupts their life. Something stops working at the worst possible time. Now the pain of waiting is greater than the pain of calling.

Now they need someone immediately. Whoever answers their call first gets the job.

You missed the call. The job is gone. In the field, on break, or too late, it doesn't matter. They've already gone with someone else. You lost a customer.

What if you reached them before they called your competitor?

You called before it disrupted their life. It's not an effort for them. Nobody wants to listen to your answering message or press 3 to get to a human. Buying your service becomes the convenient option.

The job is yours. You got to the customer before they called your competitor. You didn't just win the contract; you eliminated the race.
