Common Questions

Everything you want to know

Most people do not know the answer yet. That is what we help you figure out. We look at your teeth, gums, bone, and smile. Then we tell you if veneers, crowns, implants, or a mix of treatments makes the most sense. The goal is to give you a strong, natural-looking smile without doing more than you need.

You might be able to. Even if you have bad teeth, missing teeth, old root canals, or dentures, implants may still be an option. We check your health, gums, bone, and habits like smoking. If you need extra steps first, like bone work or gum care, we will tell you before you start.

During the visit, your mouth will be numb, and we can help you feel calm and relaxed. Many people remember little or nothing from the visit. We offer sedation options and watch your breathing, heart rate, and blood pressure the whole time. Afterward, you may feel sore, but most people manage it with medicine and clear care instructions.

Like any surgery, there are risks. You could have swelling, infection, slow healing, or an implant that does not attach to the bone. Rarely, there can be nerve or sinus problems. We use 3D scans to plan carefully, lower the risks, and explain what matters for your case.

Dental implants can last many years, sometimes even a lifetime, if you take care of them. Crowns and veneers can also last a long time, often 10 to 15 years or more. You will need to brush, floss, use the right cleaning tools, and come in for checkups so we can keep everything healthy.

Full mouth treatment costs more because it takes careful planning, surgery, custom teeth, strong materials, and several visits. It is not a quick patch. It is meant to fix bigger problems for the long term. We give you a clear written plan, show you the costs, talk about payment options, and help you understand what makes the most sense for your mouth.
